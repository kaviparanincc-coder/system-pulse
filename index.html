What is this
System Pulse is a daily body intelligence journal.
It was built from a personal observation: we go through each day reacting to how we feel, without ever pausing to actually name it. We push through fogginess, ignore stomach discomfort, and dismiss physical tiredness — and then wonder why some days feel impossible while others feel effortless.

System Pulse changes that. Three short check-ins per day. Three scores. No judgment. Just honest self-observation.
The philosophy
Awareness is the first form of care.
You don't need to fix everything every day. But you do need to know how you are. Most people have never systematically tracked whether their mind, gut, and body tend to feel better or worse on certain days, after certain foods, or after certain habits.

This app gives you a mirror — not a coach, not a calculator. Just a quiet daily mirror. Over weeks, patterns emerge. Your own patterns. And that awareness naturally starts to guide better choices — without rules or commands.
The science behind it
Mind, gut, and body are one connected system.
The gut is often called the second brain. It contains over 100 million neurons and produces 90% of the body's serotonin. When your gut is off, your mood follows. When your body is stiff, your mind is more foggy. When your mind is anxious, your stomach tightens.

The System Health Index (SHI) reflects this — a weighted score that acknowledges the mind's central role while accounting for body and gut as equal contributors to how a day actually feels.
The morning ritual
A slow start is not a luxury — it's a strategy.
Movement first (wake the body), then meditation (settle the mind), then a gentle breakfast (signal calm to the gut). This sequence mirrors how the body's systems naturally want to activate when not rushed.

Five minutes of movement. Five minutes of stillness. One good meal. That's the whole ritual.
